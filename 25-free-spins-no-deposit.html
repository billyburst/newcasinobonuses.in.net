Understanding the Terms Behind 25 Free Spins

Before you get too excited about your free spins, it's worth understanding what happens after you've used them. Any winnings from your spins will be added to your account as bonus funds. Whether you can withdraw those winnings immediately depends on the casino's wagering requirements.

A wagering requirement tells you how many times you need to bet your winnings before you can cash them out. For example, if you win £5 from your free spins and the wagering requirement is 30x, you'd need to wager a total of £150 before you could withdraw. Some casinos have done away with wagering entirely on their no deposit spins, which means anything you win is real cash straight away.

There's usually a maximum win limit too. Even if you're lucky enough to hit a big payout during your free spins, the casino may cap your withdrawable winnings at £20, £50, or £100. Always check the specific terms at the casino you choose, as these limits vary quite a bit from one site to another.
