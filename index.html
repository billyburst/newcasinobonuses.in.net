What to Look For When Choosing a Free Spins Offer

Not all free spins offers are created equal, and it pays to read the fine print before claiming any bonus. The first thing to check is the wagering requirement. This tells you how many times you need to play through any winnings before you can withdraw them. Some casinos offer free spins with no wagering at all, meaning whatever you win is yours to keep straight away. Others may set the wagering at 20x, 35x, or even higher.

You should also check the maximum win cap. Many no deposit bonuses limit how much you can actually withdraw from your free spins winnings. A typical cap might be £20 to £100, even if you technically win more than that while playing. The game weighting matters too — usually only slots contribute 100% towards clearing the wagering requirement, while table games may contribute much less or not at all.
